A textbook photograph most likelyled me to think the Rosetta Stone the sizeof a library’s old Webster’s Third Editionor two loaves of bread on a side board,but here it stands, three tongues, or one mindthat can say three ways we say the one thing,the breaths and sights of each way in rock,a milestone in intangibles between them.Reflected light from outside through the entrance,duplicating on the glass case      the doorimage that the stone itself is       openswhen you walk around behind it exhibitthe inhibition of letters, and I see you,not a translation, step through from beyond all descriptioninto the calling of flesh in black skin:beauty. Beauty. Beauty.
