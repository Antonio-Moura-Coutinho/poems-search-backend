



After Seferis



Since we still had a littleOf the rusk left, what foolsTo eat, against the rules,The Sun’s slow-moving cattle,Each ox huge as a tank — A wall you’d have to siegeFor forty years to reachA star, a hero’s rank.We starved on the back of the earth,But when we’d stuffed ourselves,We tumbled to these delves,Numbskulls, fed up with dearth.
