Whatever country, I mean, where inconceivablewas a word like any other lies far behind me now. I’velearned to spare what’s failing, if it can keep what’s livingalive still, maybe just                                       awhile longer. Ghost bamboo thatthe birds nest in, for example, not noticing the leaves, colorof surrender, color of poverty as I used to imagine it whenI myself was poor but had no idea of it. I’ve always thoughtgratitude’s the one correct response to having been made,however painfully, to see this life more up close.
