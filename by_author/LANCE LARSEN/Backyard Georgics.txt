
It takes a calendar one damp day to declare fall,weeks of dying mums to second the motion.                               *     *     * Gone the homeland, gone the father, nothing leftbut invisible north to magnetize your doubts.                               *     *     * Not eulogies or hearses but the sandwiches after,estranged cousins chewing under one umbrella.                               *     *     * One clock for errands, one for midnighttrysts, though neither will hurry a slow train.                               *     *     * Prairie is not the floor nor sky the coffered ceiling.Even a scarecrow is wise beyond its straw.                               *     *     *Look down: a river of grass. Look up: a velvet lostand found. Look inside: no straws to drink that dusk.                               *     *     *A woman’s watch thieved by a jay—ah, to be liftedlike that, to be carried like time across lapping waves.
