
No one can draw fast enoughTo capture the cutIris before its form fallsFrom its former self.But when we passed a patchIn the ditch,She told me to stop and she steppedDown, opening her claspKnife. She spared one irisWith an impressionisticCocoon on its stemAnd cut the flower beside it.Once homeShe rendered in a careful hurry.She drew into the night as the iris died.I woke grafted to herIn a vague, translucent hammock of dread.
