
The boat was beating across the bay,we had our backs to Vesuvius,the wind smacked our faces.Naples was an enormous packet of cigarettesyou could smoke till you conked out:the cigarettes were never going to run outand nor was the coffee, the drugs,the prostitutes, the locked churches,the scooters, the rice cakes, the evil eye,the boys called Gennaro, the funiculars,the shrines to Madonna, the shrinesto Maradona, the bullet holes, the heat,the permanent state of crucifixion.Anyone could be crucified two thousandyears ago but to be crucified now,to be crucified in Napoli — lift me up!
