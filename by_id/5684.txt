
Only this boy movesbetween the runes of treeson his tricyclewhen an eagle swoops,releases two arrowsfrom its silver wings, and meltsaway faster than lightning.Then a loud whistleand a bang like dry thunder.In a blink the boy seeshis house roof sink.Feels his ears ripped off.The blast puffs up a fawn smokebigger than a mountain cloud.The slow begonias rattletheir scarlet like confetti.Metal slashesthe trees and ricochets.Wires and pipes snapat the roots, quiver.The whirling smoke packedwith bricks and cement,chicken feathers and nigella seeds.When the cloud beginsto settle on the ground,the boy makes out buckled iron rods.White soot descendsand he finds himself dressedlike an apprentice baker.
