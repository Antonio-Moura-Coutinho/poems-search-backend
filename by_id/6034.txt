



carnegie hall, october 19, 2014



beauty eludes me, usually. i soak up the lush red, violet, indigo bloomsabdullah ibrahim’s cool fingers pluck from the keyboard’s bed, but bring to these ‘rooms’(stanzas forged from replayed past as today’s not-news) no solacing bouquets. my weeds?i conjure rough green to rupture from seeds so furious they bleed — or, grieving, raisecrabgrass and blue notes, peppered with rust, where he grows flowers. yes, i tend my plantsincisively : no phrase that droops or wants out of the sun survives long. but the restrun wild, flush vivid, throw shade, deluge fruit,
lavishly express their dissonant root.
