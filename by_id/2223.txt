
I could almost hear their soft collisions on the cold air today, but when I came in,shed my layers and stood alone by the fire, I felt them float toward me like sporesflung far from their source, having crossed miles of oceans and fields unknown to mostjust to keep my body fixed to its place on the earth. Call them God if you must,these messengers that bring hard evidence of what I once was and where I have been—filling me with bits of stardust, whaleskin, goosedown from the pillow where Einsteinonce slept, tucked in his cottage in New Jersey, dreaming of things I know I’ll never see.
